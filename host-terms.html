Host Terms of Participation

What it means to host a Hearth drop

What a host is

A host is a venue, organisation, club, school, or individual that provides a location, audience, or occasion for a Hearth drop. Hosts are partners in the Hearth model — not passive locations. These terms cover your role as a host.

What Hearth provides to hosts

As a host, you get:

  • A simple, structured way to organise food moments for your members, guests, or community
  • A customer-facing ordering experience that handles pre-orders before the event
  • Operational tools that reduce the burden of organising food on the day
  • Where agreed with the vendor, a share of proceeds or a fundraising contribution from each drop
  • A record of drops run at your venue over time

Promoting your drop

When a vendor runs a drop at your venue, they will provide a shareable link and QR code. We ask hosts to help promote the drop through the channels that already reach your audience — WhatsApp groups, newsletters, notice boards, social posts. The more your community knows about it, the better the drop performs for everyone.

You are not required to guarantee a minimum number of orders. We ask only that you promote in good faith.

Shared value and revenue share

Where a revenue share or fundraising contribution has been agreed with the vendor, Hearth will record and track that arrangement. The exact amount and format (percentage per order, fixed contribution per drop, or donation to a named cause) is agreed between you and the vendor when the drop is set up.

Hearth facilitates this arrangement but is not party to the specific commercial terms between host and vendor. Any agreed contribution should be confirmed with the vendor directly before the drop goes live.

Data and your audience

When customers order through a Hearth drop at your venue, their data is collected and held by Hearth. Hosts do not receive raw customer data unless explicitly agreed as part of a specific arrangement.

If you share audience contact details with Hearth or a vendor to promote a drop, you confirm that you have the appropriate permission to do so. Hearth's privacy policy governs how all customer data is handled.

Your obligations on the day

Hosts are responsible for:

  • Ensuring the venue is accessible during the drop window
  • Communicating any access or logistics changes to the vendor promptly
  • Supporting a smooth customer experience on the day — clear signage, collection point access, and where possible a nominated point of contact

The vendor is responsible for food preparation, delivery, and fulfilment. Hearth is responsible for the ordering platform. Hosts are responsible for the venue and local logistics.

Ending participation

You are free to stop hosting Hearth drops at any time. We ask only that you honour any drops already scheduled and communicate any changes to the vendor as early as possible.

Hearth or the creating vendor may remove a host from the platform if participation causes harm to customers, vendors, or the integrity of the platform. We would always seek to resolve issues through conversation first.

Changes to these terms

We may update these terms as the platform evolves. We will notify you of significant changes with reasonable notice.
