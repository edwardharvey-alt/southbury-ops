How Hearth works

01

Plan a drop

Set your menu, timing, and capacity. Choose where it's hosted — a local pub, a school fundraiser, a neighbourhood delivery area, or your own premises. A drop is a planned moment. Not an always-open store. You decide when, where, and how much.

02

Your community orders in advance

Orders come in before you cook a thing. Thirty wood-fired pizzas or a butcher's weekend hamper, Friday evening, Dorchester town centre. Orders close at noon. You know exactly what's needed, so you make exactly that — no guesswork, no waste, no last-minute chaos. Customers pay upfront. You deliver with confidence.

03

Every order builds something you own

Most food businesses start from zero every service. Hearth doesn't. Every order adds to a real customer database — postcodes, purchase history, loyalty signals — that belongs to you. Not to a platform. Not shared with competitors. Yours.

04

Reach the right people before your next drop opens

Before your next drop goes live, Hearth identifies which customers are most likely to order — based on where they live, what they've bought before, and how recently they engaged. You reach the right people at the right moment, with a message that's relevant. Not a broadcast to a rented list. Not a guess. The longer you run, the smarter the system gets — and the easier each drop is to fill.
