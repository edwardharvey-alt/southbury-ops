How Hearth works

Why the model is different — and why that matters.

Hearth is built around planned drops rather than continuous ordering. Each drop is designed with a clear menu, a fixed order window and declared capacity — so food can be prepared with intent, not pressure.

That creates a calmer, more controlled way for vendors to operate. It also makes drops easier for hosts to organise, whether they are neighbourhoods, clubs, schools or other community groups.

By removing the need for expensive third-party aggregators, vendors retain more of what they earn — while hosts can use regular drops to bring people together and, where relevant, raise funds locally.

Over time, this creates something more valuable than a series of good services. Every drop adds to a customer base the vendor owns directly. That asset compounds — each drop makes the next easier to fill, more targeted and more efficient to run.
