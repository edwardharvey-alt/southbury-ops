# Hearth — Brand Playbook

**Status:** Live canon. Formal brand reference across product, design, and communications.

---

## 1. Brand definition

**Core line**

> Hearth enables planned food moments, shared locally.

**Supporting line**

> Built with and for the communities they serve.

**Expanded definition**

Hearth is built around planned drops — not always-on ordering. Each drop is a defined moment with a set menu, a fixed time window, and a clear capacity. This creates a calmer, more controlled way for vendors to operate, while making it simple for hosts to organise local drops and, where relevant, raise funds for their community.

---

## 2. Brand belief

> Great local food should strengthen communities, not bypass them.

This belief drives the drop model, the host model, the absence of marketplace dynamics, and the vendor-first design.

---

## 3. Brand role

Hearth **is**:

- Infrastructure for community food moments
- A vendor-first operating model
- A system for planned, capacity-led delivery

Hearth **is not**:

- A marketplace
- An algorithmic discovery engine
- An on-demand delivery platform
- A growth-at-all-costs SaaS product

---

## 4. Brand idea

**Planned local food moments.**

Hearth exists to bring rhythm back to food, make delivery feel shared rather than anonymous, and create repeatable local participation.

---

## 5. Brand personality

- **Calm** — no noise, no urgency, no chaos.
- **Assured** — confident through restraint, not hype.
- **Warm** — human, welcoming, grounded in hospitality.
- **Considered** — everything feels intentional: menus, timing, language, layout.
- **Local** — rooted in place, hosts, and repeat community engagement.

**Brand shorthand:** warm restraint + operational discipline.

---

## 6. Tone of voice

**Principles:** simple, clear, human, slightly editorial, calm and factual, commercially intelligent rather than commercial-first.

**Good examples**

- "Your next drop is scheduled for Friday evening."
- "Orders open at 5pm."
- "This drop is now full."
- "Signals are building."
- "Your drops are gaining momentum."

**Avoid**

- "Selling fast!"
- "Boost revenue now"
- "Maximise conversion"
- "Trending near you"
- "Limited-time offer"

---

## 7. Vocabulary system

**Core terms (locked):** Drop · Capacity · Menu · Host · Planned moment · Shared locally · Live / Scheduled / Draft · Service Board · Drop Studio · Offer · Brand · Insights.

**Avoid:** Campaign · Listing · Marketplace · Inventory · SKU · Funnel · Promotion · Deal · Boost · Convert · Optimise · Leverage · Trending · Algorithm.

> Note: navigation canon is **Offer** (not "Menu Library") and **Brand** (not "Brand Hearth").

---

## 8. Visual identity

**Overall feel:** natural, warm, restrained, premium (but not luxury).

**Colour palette**

- **Platform accent:** `#C4511A` — Hearthfire (`var(--h-fire)`). The primary platform accent.
- **Vendor-brand fallback:** `#8B6B3F` — retained only as `--vendor-brand-primary` fallback, not the platform accent.
- **Secondary:** `#CBB89D` — soft sand
- **Background:** `#FAF8F4` — off-white
- **Text:** `#1F2937` — near-black (brand ink)

**Typography**

- **Display:** Cormorant Garamond
- **UI:** Figtree

**Design principles:** generous spacing, rounded edges, soft borders, minimal shadows, calm card-based layouts, premium empty states.

**Imagery:** real food, real environments, natural light. No stock-photo feel.

**Visual inspiration:** wood · flour · fire · stone.

---

## 9. Brand architecture (critical)

**Two layers.**

1. **Hearth (platform layer)** — always present via layout, structure, navigation, tone, and interaction patterns.
2. **Vendor (foreground layer)** — customisable: logo, hero image, colours, identity.

**Key rule:**

> Hearth frames the experience. Vendor fills the experience.

---

## 10. Product expression

- **Service Board** — calm, operational, controlled. Language: "Next items to make", "Orders placed", "Ready".
- **Drop Studio** — intentional, structured. Language: "Create drop", "Set capacity", "Assign menu".
- **Offer** — crafted, considered. Language: "Products", "Bundles", "Capacity impact".
- **Brand** — editorial, identity-led.
- **Insights** — intelligent, supportive, non-judgemental. Language: "Signals", "Momentum", "What's working".
- **Order Page** — local, calm, premium, capacity-aware, time-bound.

---

## 11. Mutual benefit model

Hearth is designed so both sides benefit naturally.

**Vendors:** retain more revenue (no aggregator dependency), more control over operations, predictable demand, stronger local identity.

**Communities / hosts:** easy to organise drops, repeat local moments, fundraising potential, shared value tied to participation.

**Brand expression.** Never say "helping vendors and communities equally." Instead: "Vendors retain more of what they earn — while hosts can organise and benefit from local drops."

---

## 12. Guardrails (non-negotiable)

Never allow Hearth to feel like a marketplace, a feed-based discovery app, a discount platform, a growth-hacking tool, or a logistics engine.

Avoid algorithmic language, fake urgency, "trending" / "popular near you", and discounts or promotions as core messaging.

**Capacity rule.** Capacity must always feel real, respected, and operational — never manipulated or artificial.

---

## 13. Homepage brand structure

**Headline:** Hearth enables planned food moments, shared locally.
**Supporting:** Built with and for the communities they serve.
**Flow:** Brand → Menu → Drop → Run → Learn.

---

## 14. Brand expression hierarchy

1. **Emotional** — planned moments, community, belonging.
2. **Operational** — drops, capacity, time windows, menus.
3. **Commercial** — margin, efficiency, fundraising (revealed quietly).

---

## 15. What success feels like

If the brand is working: Hearth feels like a place, not a tool; vendors feel proud to use it; communities feel included and supported; customers feel part of something local; the system feels calm, structured, and repeatable.

---

## 16. One-line brand lock

> Hearth enables planned food moments, shared locally.

Everything must reinforce this.
